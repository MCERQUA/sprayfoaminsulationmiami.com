If you're researching spray foam insulation for your Miami home, cost is almost certainly your first question. It's also the question with the most "it depends" answers online. This guide gives you real numbers for South Florida, explains how pricing actually works, and shows the payback so you can decide with confidence.

## How spray foam pricing works

Spray foam is priced by the **board foot** — one square foot of area covered, one inch thick. The two things that drive your final number are the product and the total board feet:

- **Open-cell foam** typically runs **$0.50–$1.00 per board foot** installed.
- **Closed-cell foam** typically runs **$1.00–$1.75 per board foot** installed.

Closed-cell costs more because the material is denser and you usually install it at a greater thickness (2–3 inches) to hit target R-values and get a true vapor barrier. Open-cell is softer and lighter, so it covers more area per unit of material.

## Typical Miami project costs

Most insulation isn't sold by the board foot to the homeowner — it's quoted as a complete project. Here's what Miami homeowners commonly see:

- **Attic only (open-cell), single-family home:** roughly **$2,500–$5,500**.
- **Attic only (closed-cell), single-family home:** roughly **$4,500–$9,000+**, depending on thickness and whether the roof deck is included.
- **Whole-home new construction:** varies widely with square footage and product mix, often **$1.50–$4.00+ per square foot** of conditioned floor area.
- **Wall retrofit or single room:** smaller scopes, usually a half-day project.

These ranges move with accessibility (tight crawlspace vs. wide-open attic), prep work, removal of old insulation, and current material costs. The only way to know your exact number is a written, itemized estimate.

## Why closed-cell costs more — and when it's worth it

Closed-cell's higher price buys you three things open-cell can't deliver:

1. **A true vapor barrier.** In humid South Florida this matters enormously — it keeps moisture out of your walls and attic, which is the root cause of most mold problems.
2. **Higher R-value per inch (R-6.5 vs. R-3.7).** You hit target thermal performance in less depth, which matters in tight assemblies.
3. **Structural reinforcement.** Closed-cell is rigid and bonds to framing and roof decks, adding strength that's valuable in hurricane-exposed and flood-zone properties.

For Miami Beach, the barrier islands, and flood-zone properties, we almost always recommend closed-cell. For interior soundproofing or a budget-conscious attic where the assembly is designed to dry, open-cell can be the right call.

## The payback: how fast it pays for itself

This is the number that changes the conversation. Properly installed spray foam typically cuts **cooling costs by 30–50%** for Miami homes. Here's a realistic scenario:

- Miami home with monthly summer electric bills around **$300–$450**.
- Conservative **35% reduction** after spray foam.
- That's roughly **$100–$160/month** in savings, or **$1,200–$1,900/year**.
- A $4,000 attic project pays back in roughly **2.5–3.5 years**.
- After that, the savings continue for the life of the home — spray foam lasts **30 to 80+ years** without degrading.

Your actual savings depend on your home, your current insulation, and your AC usage, but for most Miami homes spray foam is one of the highest-ROI upgrades available.
