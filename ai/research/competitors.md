# Competitor Research — sprayfoaminsulationmiami.com

## Niche context (why this market is different)
South Florida is one of the **hardest contractor insurance markets in the US**. Hurricane exposure (June 1–Nov 30), HVHZ (High-Velocity Hurricane Zone) requirements in Miami-Dade, coastal salt-air corrosion, and a tightening Florida property market (Citizens depopulation, carrier withdrawals) mean SPF contractors are frequently non-renewed or face steep windstorm/named-storm deductibles. Generalist agencies routinely fail to place these risks.

## Top competitor archetypes
1. **National contractor-insurance agencies** (e.g., large online GL brokers). Strong SEO for "contractor insurance Florida" but weak on SPF-specific exposure (isocyanate/CPL, spray-rig inland marine, hurricane builders risk). Generic quote funnels, long response times.
2. **Florida local P&C agencies** selling personal + commercial lines. Local trust, but rarely specialize in SPF; often lack E&S/specialty pollution markets and can't place non-renewed contractors.
3. **Spray-foam trade program administrators** a couple of niche programs exist; decent on coverage knowledge but dated sites, thin content, weak conversion design.
4. **Big comparison/lead-gen sites** (Insureon-class). High volume, templated, no local FL voice, no hurricane/market-hardening content.

## Common gaps we exploit
- No dedicated **contractors pollution liability** emphasis for SPF (the #1 uncovered exposure).
- No **hurricane-season / named-storm / HVHZ** content for contractors.
- No coverage for **non-renewed** contractors (a huge FL pain point).
- Generic equipment coverage that ignores **salt-air corrosion and storm loss** on spray rigs.
- Slow quote turnaround; no 2-hour claims response promise.
